    When did Miranda Lambert start her career?

    While still in high school, Lambert made her professional singing debut with ‘The Texas Pride Band.’ But, before being famous, she was still honing her skills by learning new things, writing her own songs, and performing in local venues.

    She later auditioned for the USA Network singing competition in pursuit of American Idol. She advanced to the show’s final, but came in third place. Despite finishing third, she was given the opportunity to sign a contract with Sony Nashville in September.

    Lambert received financial assistance from her family to produce a CD of her first album, which led to her first professional job as the vocalist of the famed Reo Palm Isle Ballroom. In 2005, she published her debut album, “Kerosene,” which was a huge hit. Since then, her career has taken off, and she has become well-known.

    She went on to release numerous albums, including “Crazy Ex-girlfriend” in 2007, “Revolution” in September 2009, “Platinum” in 2014, and others, winning numerous awards including “Fresh Face Of Country Music Award,” “Album of the Year,” “Top New Female Vocalist Award,” and “CMA Award For Female Vocalist of the Year,” among others. Lambert debuted with the Pistol Annies, a new group made up of three members: Ashley Monroe, Angaleena Presley, and herself, on April 2, 2011.

    Lambert made her first appearances outside of the United States and Canada in March 2016 as one of the headliners at C2C: Country to Country, Europe’s largest country music festival, when she performed alongside Dwight Yoakam, Thomas Rhett, and Ashley Monroe in England, Scotland, and Ireland. The Pistol Annies rejoined after the promotion and tour for The Weight of These Wings finished, and on November 2, 2018, they released Interstate Gospel, their third studio album, which debuted at number one on the Billboard Country Albums Chart.

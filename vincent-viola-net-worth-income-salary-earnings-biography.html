    Vincent is the son of John A. Viola, an Italian immigrant, and his wife, Virginia. He graduated from Brooklyn Technical High School and was sent to the United States Military Academy, where he received a bachelor’s degree and was commissioned as a second lieutenant in the United States Army. After that, he went to Infantry Officer Basic Course and Ranger School before joining the 101st Airborne Division at Fort Campbell, where he spent the following several years. He earned a juris doctor degree from New York Law School after leaving the service, but he failed the bar test. In the early 1980s, he began his business career by accumulating $10,000 to buy a seat on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Vincent’s role at NYMEX grew over time, and he became vice president in 1993, a post he maintained until 1996.

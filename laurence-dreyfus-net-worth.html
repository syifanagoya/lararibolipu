    FAQs about Laurence Dreyfus

    Who is Laurence Dreyfus?

    He is an acclaimed American musicologist and viola da gamba player, known for his expertise in Baroque music and his scholarly work on composers like J.S. Bach and Richard Wagner.

    What is Dreyfus known for?

    He is renowned for his interpretations of Bach and Wagner, his leadership of the viol consort Phantasm, and his influential publications in musicology.

    Where did Dreyfus study?

    Dreyfus studied cello at the Juilliard School, viol at the Royal Conservatoire in Brussels, and musicology at Columbia University, where he earned his PhD.

    What books has Laurence Dreyfus written?

    Some of his key publications include Bach’s Continuo Group, Bach and the Patterns of Invention, and Wagner and the Erotic Impulse.

    Has Laurence Dreyfus won any awards?

    Yes, Dreyfus has received several awards, including the Gramophone Award for his recordings with Phantasm and the Otto Kinkeldey Award from the American Musicological Society for Bach and the Patterns of Invention.

    What is Phantasm?

    Phantasm is a viol consort founded by Dreyfus in 1994, known for their performances and recordings of early music, especially English consort music.

    Where has Laurence Dreyfus taught?

    Dreyfus has held academic positions at Yale, the University of Chicago, Stanford University, and King’s College London. He was also a Professor of Music at the University of Oxford until his retirement.

    What are Dreyfus’s research interests?

    His research focuses on 18th- and 19th-century music, particularly the works of Bach and Wagner, English consort music, theories of musical analysis, and performance studies.

    When did Dreyfus retire?

    He retired from his teaching position at Oxford in 2015 and continues to engage in independent research and performance.

    Where is Laurence Dreyfus now?

    After retiring, he relocated to Berlin, where he remains active in the music community, performing and pursuing research.
